Physiotherapy Treatments

We use a range of physiotherapy techniques from Manual Therapy to Electrotherapy (such as ultrasound, laser or interferential treatment), and develop exercise programs following individualised client assessment. Our focus is on the reduction of pain and correction of dysfunction to allow lasting relief from symptoms. We treat a range of conditions as outlined below, click on each for more information.

Musculoskeletal conditions comprise more than 150 diagnoses that affect the locomotor system; that is, muscles, bones, joints and associated tissues such as tendons and ligaments. They range from those that arise suddenly and are short-lived, such as fractures, sprains and strains, to lifelong conditions associated with ongoing pain and disability.

Musculoskeletal conditions are typically characterised by pain (often persistent) and limitations in mobility, dexterity and functional ability, reducing peopleâ€™s ability to work and participate in social roles with associated impacts on mental wellbeing, and at a broader level impacts on the prosperity of communities. The most common and disabling musculoskeletal conditions are osteoarthritis, back and neck pain, fractures associated with bone fragility, injuries and systemic inflammatory conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis.

Musculoskeletal conditions include conditions that affect:

  • joints, such as osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, gout, ankylosing spondylitis;
  • bones, such as osteoporosis, osteopenia and associated fragility fractures, traumatic fractures;
  • muscles, such as sarcopenia;
  • the spine, such as back and neck pain;
  • multiple body areas or systems, such as regional and widespread pain disorders and inflammatory diseases such as connective tissue diseases and vasculitis that have musculoskeletal manifestations, for example systemic lupus erythematosus.

Musculoskeletal conditions are prevalent across the life-course and most commonly affect people from adolescence through to older age. Here at Abbey Physiotherapy we offer a range of treatment options for these conditions. If you wish to discuss your symptoms in more detail please get in touch today.

We treat injuries from a wide range of sports here at Abbey Physiotherapy, from GAA to running to rugby and everything in between. Common sports injuries can be categorised according to how they happened:

  • Trauma: direct trauma such as a blow to the thigh can cause bleeding and inflammation in the muscle(dead leg). Falling on an outstretched arm could result in a shoulder dislocation or wrist fracture. Indirect trauma as in landing awkwardly in a tackle could result in a ligament rupture (cruciate tear), muscle tear (hamstring) or a torn cartilage.
  • Overuse: repetitive actions can strain muscles, ligaments and tendons. Common in sports with repetitive throwing or gripping such as tennis and golf. Sports such as football with a strong rotary action can result in recurrent hip and groin pain.
  • Biomechanical: our body type has a strong bearing on our ability to cope with the demands made by sporting actions. In dynamic activities, for example, the foot is the propeller. Poor alignment of the foot will cause undue strain on the joints of the lower limb and lumbar spine.

We work close with local clubs and groups and offer discounted treatment options to members. We also provide strapping and bandaging services pre-match/training session as required.If you wish to discuss your injury in more detail please get in touch today.

Rheumatological conditions are autoimmune diseases which cause inflammation to joints, muscles, tendons or ligaments. These conditions include, but are not limited to, Rheumatoid Arthritis, Osteoarthritis & Fibromyalgia amongst others. Rheumatological physiotherapy is an excellent way of managing these symptoms and pain associated with conditions and reducing the effect that rheumatological conditions and improving quality of life. There a range of treatment options available depending on the conidtion and patient, if you wish to discuss your symptoms in more detail please get in touch today.

Many people, of all ages, have issues with their posture, which can exacerbate pain, exitsing conditions and cause injury. Common postural abnormalities include rounded or hunched shoulders, forward head, rounded upper back, spine curvature, anterior or posterior pelvic tilt, foot supination (high arch) or pronation (flat foot). Everyday activities such as sitting at a desk, carrying young children, using smartphones and even some sporting activities can increase your risk of developing postural issues.

Our physios will undertake a full assessment of your posture and identify what factors are influencing it before providing a treatment plan to carefully correct any abnormalities reducing risk of worsening postural complaints and future injury.

We offer physio support and rehabilitation for a range of traumatic injuries including treatment for conditions such as strokes, brain injuries, lower leg, upper extremity, spinal fractures & whiplash.

Many patients suffer from chronic pain due to a variety of conditions. These can be reflex sympathetic dystrophy, severe low back pain, whiplash, fibromyalgia, phantom limb pain, arthritis, or nerve injuries amongst many others. Living with chronic pain can severly limit quality of life for many patients and cause considerable emotional distress. Physiotherapy helps people with long term, chronic pain develop the skills and treatment prpgramme they need to manage their condition, increase their activity and improve quality of life.

Preparing for surgery can often speed up post surgery recovery times. This can include increased activity and targeted strengthening exercises prior to surgery. We can undertake a pre surgery assessment and identify a plan to help get you in the best possible physical shape for surgery and help you mentally prepare for what to expect post surgery.

Post surgery we can offer immediate treatment to reduce pain and stiffness and provide you with a longer term rehabilitation programme to return you to full mobility as soon as safely possible.

Personal Training

At Abbey Physiotherapy we offer personal training to individual patients. Using all this combined with bodyweight and cardio workouts we will help you get in your best shape.
